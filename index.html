HAZ

Large Language Models (LLMs) struggle with verifiability and hallucination in planning, preventing real-world deployment. Our mission is the LLM-as-Formalizer paradigm: shifting the LLM's role from an unreliable planner to a precise translator. The LLM converts user goals into rigorous formal languages (like PDDL), which are then solved by symbolic solvers, guaranteeing verifiability and deterministic execution.

I. Tool-Driven Problem Solving

This primary line enhances framework robustness by mapping complex reasoning tasks—including MDP/POMDP Planning, Deductive Reasoning, and Constraint Satisfaction—to the correct external solvers. We employ formal tools like PDDL, SMT, and Lean as verifiable intermediate representations. This research extends to Iterative Formalization for dynamic, partially observable environments and Multimodal Formalization from sensor data.

II. Optimization and Model Interpretability

This secondary line focuses on optimizing the efficiency and fidelity of these neuro-symbolic tools. We refine the LLM's translation process via advanced Generative Chain-of-Thought (CoT) methods and novel intermediate formal languages. Crucially, we apply Mechanistic Interpretability (Mech-Int) techniques to dissect how the model translates language, ensuring long-term reliability.
